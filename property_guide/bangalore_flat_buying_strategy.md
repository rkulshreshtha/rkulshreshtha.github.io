# Strategy for Buying a Good Flat in Bengaluru (Without Getting Robbed)

Extracted from the r/indianrealestate thread discussing strategies for buying end-use family apartments in Bengaluru.

| Key Strategy / Point | Reasoning / Ground Reality |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Target 10+ Year Old Tier-1 Properties** | Older constructions from reputed builders (Sobha, Brigade, etc.) offer better carpet area ratios (80-85% vs. modern 70-75%). You avoid the bloated "new launch" premiums funded by heavy marketing and EOIs. |
| **Seek NRI Sellers** | NRIs settled abroad often want a hassle-free exit and are less likely to demand black money components. They are generally more open to significant negotiations (15-20 Lakhs) as their livelihood does not depend on the sale. |
| **Use Unconventional Search Channels** | The best deals are often off-market. Instead of relying solely on portals, activate friends in target societies and offer incentives to security guards or association managers for leads on desperate sellers. |
| **Check Actual Registry Prices** | Do not anchor negotiations to portal asking prices (MagicBricks/99acres), which are often artificially inflated by sellers "testing their luck". Check actual SRO/registry data to find the true market clearing price. |
| **Structural & Plumbing Due Diligence** | The biggest risk with 10-15 year old properties is severe plumbing and seepage issues. Buyers must assess if the issue is isolated to the flat (fixable for ~2-3L) or a systemic society failure. Using services like Nemmadi is recommended. |
| **Section 195 IT Act Compliance (NRI TDS)** | When purchasing from an NRI, the buyer is legally obligated to deduct TDS (often >20%) and submit it. If you fail to do this, the buyer is held liable for the tax burden. |
| **Patience and "The Elimination Method"** | Finding a fair resale deal takes time (often 6-12 months). The secondary market is a buyer's market. Eliminate rude or arrogant sellers and brokers immediately; focus on humble sellers whose properties have sat unsold for 4-5 months. |
| **Treat it as a Final Home, Not an Investment** | When buying a decade-old property for end-use, the focus should be on lifestyle, amenities, and emotional release rather than aggressive capital appreciation or redevelopment prospects (which are rare for high-rises in BLR). |
