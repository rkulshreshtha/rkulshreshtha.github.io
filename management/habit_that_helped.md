# Impactful People-Management Habits

Extracted from the r/managers community discussion on small habits that genuinely make you a better leader.

| Management Habit | Subsequent Actions | Why it was helpful | Additional Information |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The 'One Word' Check-in** | Start 1:1s asking 'What’s one word that describes your state today?' Go first if they hesitate. | Opens the door to honest conversations and grounds the meeting. | Sounds cheesy, but works well to break the ice. |
| **Quarterly 'Hate' Audit** | Once a quarter, ask the team what they hate doing most, then work to eliminate or improve that process. | Surfaces friction and inefficiencies without making people feel like they're just complaining. | Huge morale booster; prevents silent frustrations from building up. |
| **High/Low/Priorities 1:1s** | Structure 1:1s around 3 points: highest point last week, lowest point last week, priorities for next week. | Easily aligns goals, uncovers roadblocks early, and intentionally celebrates wins. | Great alternative for people tired of generic status updates. |
| **Human-First Openers** | Start conversations with 'How's your week actually going?' or 'What's on your mind?' before jumping to tasks. | Shifts people out of performance mode, uncovers early blockers, and builds trust. | Helps avoid robotic interactions; can be adapted for chatty vs. quiet teams. |
| **The Power of the Pause** | Take a mental step back when emotionally triggered. Don't try to solve a problem upon its first mention. | Helps both the manager and the team be less reactive and more intentional during crises. | Pairs perfectly with active listening. |
| **Teaching to Fish (Span of Control)** | Stop doing the work for them; walk them through the solution. Let go of issues outside your direct control. | Prevents manager burnout, empowers the team, and stops managers from hoarding work. | Treat employment as transactional, but remember you're dealing with humans who have lives. |
| **Daily Rounds (Greetings & Goodbyes)** | Walk around to say good morning to everyone and goodbye as they leave (or equivalent async check-ins). | Creates informal opportunities for employees to bring up small issues before they become big ones. | Even quick midweek async check-ins work well for remote teams. |
| **Inclusive Question Phrasing** | Ask 'What have I missed?' or 'What questions do you have?' instead of a closed 'Any questions?'. | Assumes there is more to uncover and makes the team feel safer speaking up. | Small phrasing tweaks have massive psychological impacts on a team's willingness to share. |
| **Timely Positive Feedback** | Give positive feedback immediately when expected work is done well. Prioritize timely feedback over perfect feedback. | Builds respect, making tough/negative feedback much easier to deliver when needed. | Catching and praising great work in the moment encourages them to repeat the behavior. |
| **The 'Support' Question** | After discussing solutions to a problem, explicitly ask, 'How can I support you in this?' | Validates the employee's agency while ensuring they know you have their back. | Even if they reply 'I've got it from here,' the act of asking matters. |
